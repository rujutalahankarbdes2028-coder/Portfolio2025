The Women of Dadar Flower Market

This project is a feature magazine article centered around the resilient women of Mumbai’s Dadar Flower Market — the often-overlooked backbone of one of the city’s most vibrant, sensory spaces. While countless stories capture the color and chaos of the market itself, few delve into the lives of the women who sustain it.

This article aimed to spotlight these women — not just as vendors, but as breadwinners, entrepreneurs, and community anchors. The final outcome was a feature-length article that combined journalism and cultural commentary, celebrating the strength and independence of the women of Dadar Flower Market. The piece not only met the academic criteria but was also recognized for its depth of research and authentic portrayal of its subjects.

Role

As both the researcher and writer, I led the entire process — from conceptualization to final narrative.

This multifaceted role required both journalistic rigor and creative empathy, blending research-based insights with narrative craft.

Category

Editorial Writing / Journalism

Project type

Academic Assignment

Objective

The goal of this piece was to humanize the narrative surrounding the Dadar Flower Market by focusing on the women who drive it. I wanted to explore how their daily labor, resilience, and economic independence shape both their families and the market’s culture. By bringing forward their voices, I sought to challenge the limited representation of women in informal labor sectors and to highlight their quiet but powerful contributions to Mumbai’s urban fabric.

Research Process

My process began with in-person field research, visiting the Dadar Flower Market multiple times during different hours of the day to understand the rhythms of their work. I conducted informal interviews and conversations with several women vendors; documenting their stories, routines, and challenges.
This primary research was supported by secondary research; studying existing articles, market histories, and economic studies on women in Mumbai’s informal workforce. This dual approach helped me situate personal stories within a larger social and economic context.

Writing Process

I organized the narrative around recurring themes that emerged from the interviews resilience, community, and economic agency. I aimed for a tone that was observational yet empathetic, allowing the women’s voices to take center stage while contextualizing their experiences in a broader socio-economic landscape.
The structure moved fluidly between storytelling and commentary, grounding each personal vignette in wider systemic observations.
