UX Research Study

This project explored the user experience of the Uber app from the perspective of Gen Z women, focusing on their interaction patterns, emotional responses, and perceptions of safety. While Uber is one of the most widely used ride-hailing apps globally, its user experience often varies across demographic groups. The study aimed to understand how Gen Z women — as a digitally fluent yet safety-conscious audience — perceive and engage with Uber’s interface and services.
The goal was to uncover insights into the needs, concerns, and motivations of Gen Z female users, particularly around ease of use, trust, and personal safety within the app experience. The project sought to identify opportunities for improving Uber’s design and communication strategies to better serve this demographic, making their interaction smoother, more intuitive, and emotionally reassuring.

Role

As the UX Researcher, I was responsible for designing and executing the research process.
This role required both analytical precision and empathetic understanding, ensuring that user voices were authentically represented and that insights were grounded in real experiences.

Process

The research combined qualitative and quantitative methods to capture both personal stories and broader patterns. I conducted interviews with Gen Z women to understand their in-app behavior, safety perceptions, and emotional journey when using Uber. This qualitative data was then supported by a survey of 100 participants, helping to validate patterns around usability, satisfaction, and safety features.
The collected insights were translated into personas, empathy maps, and customer touchpoint diagrams to visualize user needs and friction points across their journey. These findings revealed opportunities to strengthen trust-building cues, improve accessibility of safety tools, and enhance communication at key interaction moments.
